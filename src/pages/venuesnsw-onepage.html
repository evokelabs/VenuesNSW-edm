NSW CRICKET CAPTAIN, AMY SATTERHWAITER, GENEROUSLY DONATES TO THE BRADMAN MUSEUM IN BOWRAL

New Zealand women's cricket captain, Amy Satterthwaite, has the world-famous Bradman Museum in Australia with a generous donation Of her match worn ODI uniform, from the recent series against Australia this summer.

Australia won the series, which makes Satterthwaite's gift all the more gracious and demonstrates the spirit of cricket.

Amy became the captain Of the New Zealand's 'VVhite Ferns' in October 2018. An all-rounder Of exceptional Skill, Amy has played 119 ODIs end 99 T20s for New Zeeland. In the 2016/17 season she became only the 2nd player, behind Kumar Sengakkare, to score four ODI centuries in a row.

Bradman Museum'S Executive Director, Rina said acknowledge generous donation as it is important to grow our contemporary artefacts within our collection. There has been a historical gap, and the museum's objective is to be a trusted source, central archive end authoritative resource for Australian cricket, end the history of women's cricket is a vital facet of the game's heritage."

To assist with this work, notable former Australian players, Lisa Sthalekar and Karen Hill (nee Price) are heading up a Women's Cricket Heritage project for the museum which includes Volunteers and former Australian and NSW players. One of the outcomes of this research will be to build a public display in time for the ICC T20 World Cup, held in Australia throughout February and March next year.

The Women's Cricket Heritage Project team. launched 2018, are currently undertaking the vital work of researching, cataloging, preserving end digitising the history Of women's cricket, meeting monthly at the Bradman Museum, in Bowral Australia.

The Executive Director added "The quality quantity Of women'S cricket related Objects has significantly over the past 3 years and we have catalogued in excess Of 1500 items in the past 12 months. The work to date been funded by the Bradman museum end recently the NSW government. We would welcome support, Volunteers or donations to assist with this vital work."

Amy uniform will be on display from April in the museum's International Cricket Hall Of Fame.
